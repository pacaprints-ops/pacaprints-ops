## 8. First-Time Buyer Guide

### Lifetime ISA (LISA) — Open One Now ⚠️ Time Sensitive
- Government adds **25% bonus** on up to £4,000/year = up to **£1,000 free per person per year**
- Must be open for **12 months** before you can use it for a house purchase
- If you open one in **April 2026**, it's eligible for **April 2027** — exactly your timeline
- Property must be **under £450,000** to use it
- **Both Carrie and partner are first-time buyers and under 40** — both open one = up to **£2,000 bonus combined**
- Carrie is 34 — has until 40 to open one, but sooner = more bonus years
- Even opening with £1 now starts the clock — add more when you can
- Provider options: Moneybox, AJ Bell, Hargreaves Lansdown (stocks & shares) or Skipton BS (cash)
- > ⚠️ Action: Both open a LISA this week. Most time-sensitive item on the entire list.

### Rental History — Strong Position
- Currently renting 8 years at same address
- Renting since age 18 — 16 years total
- This is **excellent** evidence for Skipton Track Record mortgage
- Keep bank statements showing rent going out — lender will want to see these

### 100% Mortgage — What to Know
- Very limited products available — main one is **Skipton Building Society Track Record Mortgage**
- Requirements typically include:
  - 12+ months of on-time rent payments (you'll have this)
  - Clean credit history (working toward this)
  - Proof you can afford the mortgage (affordability check)
  - No missed payments on existing credit
- Higher interest rate than mortgages with a deposit — factor into monthly budget planning
- Speak to a **specialist mortgage broker**, not just your bank — they know which lenders will consider your income setup

### Affordability — How Lenders Will Assess You
- Typically lend **4–4.5× household income**
- Combined income ~£69k → potential mortgage **£276k–£310k**
- **Important:** Carrie changing to director salary structure in May 2026 complicates this
  - Many lenders want **2–3 years of accounts or SA302s** for director/self-employed income
  - SA302 is the official HMRC tax calculation from your self-assessment — first one available after Jan 2027 filing
  - Some lenders will use just 1 year if the rest of the application is strong — broker can advise
  - Partner's PAYE income is straightforward and easier to evidence
- Existing credit commitments (cards, car) reduce how much lenders will offer — paying cards down helps

### Documents to Gather
Start collecting these now so you're not scrambling:
- [ ] Last 3 months payslips (both)
- [ ] Last 3–6 months bank statements (both — joint and personal)
- [ ] P60s for last 2 years
- [ ] SA302 from HMRC (available after filing Jan 2027)
- [ ] Proof of rental payments (12+ months — may need landlord letter or bank statements showing rent going out)
- [ ] Photo ID and proof of address
- [ ] If using LISA — confirmation of account and balance
