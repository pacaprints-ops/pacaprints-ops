## The Core Question

Should **Paca Planners** have its own social media presence (TikTok, Instagram), or should it live under the Paca Prints accounts?

There's no universally right answer — it depends on capacity, content style, and how different the audience really is.

---

## Option A — Keep Everything Under Paca Prints

Planners are promoted on the existing Paca Prints TikTok and Instagram. Paca Planners is a product range, not a separate brand in the eyes of customers.

**Pros**
- No extra accounts to manage — less work for Vicky on socials
- Existing followers already see the content
- One audience to grow instead of splitting effort between two
- Cross-sell opportunity — someone who buys a print might also buy a planner
- Lower risk — test the market without committing to a separate brand

**Cons**
- Paca Prints followers came for prints/cards/gifts — planners content might feel off-topic to them
- Harder to build a dedicated planner audience
- If Paca Planners takes off, migrating to its own account later is messy (starting from zero, confusing followers)
- Algorithm may not know what the account "is" if content themes are very mixed

---

## Option B — Separate Paca Planners Accounts from Day One

Paca Planners has its own TikTok and Instagram, distinct from Paca Prints.

**Pros**
- Clean, focused content for a focused audience (meal planners → busy people/families; wedding planner → couples)
- Algorithm can clearly categorise and push the account to the right people
- Easier to build a brand identity just for planners
- No dilution of the Paca Prints feed
- If Paca Planners grows into something bigger (more planners, SaaS etc.) the foundation is already there

**Cons**
- More work — two accounts to post on, film for, maintain
- Starting from zero followers on new accounts
- Risk of spreading thin if capacity is already tight
- If planners don't take off, two half-active accounts look worse than one active one

---

## Option C — Hybrid: Paca Prints Now, Split Later

Start by promoting Paca Planners on Paca Prints accounts to test demand. If/when it gains traction, spin up dedicated Paca Planners accounts and migrate.

**Pros**
- Low commitment up front
- Validates demand before investing in a second brand
- Buys time to decide without losing momentum

**Cons**
- "We'll split later" often doesn't happen — inertia is real
- If you do split later, early content is stranded on the wrong account
- Followers built on Paca Prints won't automatically follow the new account

---

## Website: Same Question Applies

Same decision exists for the website:

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Page on pacaprints.com** | `/planners` or similar — one site, less to manage |
| **Subdomain** | `planners.pacaprints.com` — still one site technically, but feels more separate |
| **Separate site** | `pacaplanners.com` — full independence, more work and cost |

Currently the meal planner is being added as a page on pacaprints.com, which is sensible for now regardless of what's decided on socials.

---

## Things to Consider When Deciding

1. **How different is the audience?**
   - Paca Prints: gifts, cards, personalised items — broad, everyday buyers
   - Paca Planners: people who want to get organised — could be same people, or different
   - If it's largely the same audience, keeping it together makes more sense

2. **How much content can we realistically produce?**
   - Vicky manages socials — is there capacity to run two accounts well?
   - A neglected account is worse than no account

3. **How big could Paca Planners get?**
   - If it stays as two or three planners: probably fine under Paca Prints
   - If the plan is to build it into something bigger (subscription, app, SaaS): own brand from the start makes sense

4. **What does the name "Paca Planners" suggest?**
   - It already sounds like its own thing — which points towards it having its own identity eventually
